# AI Writing for Video Marketing: Scripts That Convert

Video dominates digital content. By 2026, video accounts for over 82% of all internet traffic, and marketers who use video grow revenue 49% faster than those who don't. But here's the gap: while everyone knows video works, most struggle with the script — the foundation that determines whether a video converts viewers into customers or just entertains them briefly before they scroll on.

AI writing tools can transform your video script process, helping you create structured, compelling scripts in a fraction of the time. But video scripts have unique demands that differentiate them from written content. This guide covers the specific techniques for using AI to write video scripts that don't just get watched — they get results.

## How Video Scripts Differ from Written Content

Understanding the differences between video and written content is essential for effective AI-assisted script writing.

### Time Constraints Are Absolute

A blog post can be any length. A video script must fit a specific duration. A 60-second video allows approximately 150-160 words of spoken content. A 3-minute video allows about 450 words. Every word must justify its time cost. AI tends to generate more content than a time slot allows, so you must specify duration constraints in your prompts.

### Visual-Verbal Integration

Video scripts must account for what the audience sees as well as what they hear. The visual elements and spoken words should complement each other, not repeat each other. When someone says "this chart shows" while displaying the chart, the verbal and visual are redundant. Effective scripts use words to add context, emotion, and insight to what's shown visually.

### The First 3 Seconds

Video viewers decide in the first three seconds whether to keep watching. Unlike written content where readers might skim ahead, video viewers who bounce are gone. Your opening hook must be immediate and compelling. AI can generate hook options, but test them rigorously — the difference between a 30% and 60% retention rate at the 3-second mark determines whether the rest of your script matters.

## Video Script Structures That Convert

These proven structures give AI the framework it needs to produce effective video scripts.

### The Hook-Problem-Solution-CTA Structure

The most reliable conversion structure for short-form video. Open with an attention hook (3 seconds), define the problem (10 seconds), present the solution (30 seconds), and close with a call to action (5 seconds). AI can generate scripts within this structure when you provide the key elements.

Prompt AI: "Write a 60-second video script using Hook-Problem-Solution-CTA structure. Hook: [attention-grabbing statement]. Problem: [audience pain point]. Solution: [product/approach]. CTA: [specific action]. Include visual cues for each section. Word count: maximum 155 words."

### The Story Structure

For longer videos, narrative structure drives engagement. Set the scene, introduce a character facing a challenge, show their journey, and reveal the transformation. Story scripts work because viewers stay to find out what happens. AI can generate story-driven scripts when you provide the character, challenge, and resolution.

### The Demonstration Structure

Product demonstration videos should follow a clear before-during-after structure. Show the problem state, demonstrate the product in action, and reveal the improved result. AI can help script demonstrations that are thorough without being tedious.

## Platform-Specific Script Strategies

Each video platform has different audience behaviors and algorithm preferences. Here's how to adapt your AI scripts for the major platforms.

### YouTube Scripts

YouTube audiences expect depth and value. Videos can be longer (5-15 minutes for most content types), but they must deliver on the title's promise. Structure YouTube scripts with clear sections, include timestamps in the description, and front-load value so viewers don't click away. AI can help structure longer scripts with clear section breaks and progression.

### TikTok and Instagram Reels Scripts

Short-form video demands extreme conciseness. Every second must earn its place. Open with the hook in the first second. Deliver value immediately. End with a CTA that feels natural, not forced. AI can generate short-form scripts, but edit ruthlessly — if a word doesn't add value, cut it.

### LinkedIn Video Scripts

LinkedIn audiences expect professional, insight-driven content. Videos should deliver clear business value. Avoid entertainment for its own sake. AI can help draft LinkedIn scripts that balance professionalism with engagement.

## Optimizing Video Scripts with AI

Beyond initial drafting, AI can help optimize your video scripts for better performance.

### Hook Testing

Generate 10-15 different opening hooks for the same video concept. Test them as short clips or in thumbnail text to see which drives the highest click-through rate. The hook that wins becomes your video opening.

### CTA Optimization

Test different calls to action: direct commands ("subscribe now"), value propositions ("get the free guide"), questions ("what's your biggest challenge?"), and curiosity gaps ("I'll show you the results in a second"). AI can generate CTA variations quickly for testing.

### Script Compression

Ask AI to compress your script without losing key messages. "Reduce this script from 200 words to 160 words while preserving all key messages and the emotional arc." Compression forces prioritization and often produces tighter, more impactful scripts.

## Practical Tips for AI Video Scripts

1. **Always specify video duration in your prompt.** AI will generate the right amount of content when it knows the time constraint.
2. **Include visual cues in your scripts.** Note what should be shown on screen alongside each spoken section.
3. **Read scripts aloud before filming.** Spoken language is different from written language. If it sounds awkward when spoken, rewrite it.
4. **Test hooks independently.** The hook determines whether anyone sees the rest of your script. Invest disproportionate effort here.
5. **Keep CTAs simple and specific.** "Click the link in the description" outperforms "learn more."
6. **Generate multiple script versions.** Different approaches work for different audiences. Create variations and test.
7. **Include pause and emphasis cues.** Help the speaker deliver the script naturally by marking where to slow down, speed up, or emphasize.
