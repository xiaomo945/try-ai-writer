## Introduction

Most AI writing tools are built for marketers and bloggers, but what if you are a novelist, screenwriter, or storyteller? Two platforms that cater to creative writers are Try AI Writer and Sudowrite. While Try AI Writer is a general-purpose tool with creative capabilities, Sudowrite is purpose-built for fiction and narrative writing. This comparison explores how each one performs when the goal is not SEO or conversions — it is great storytelling.

For a broader look at AI tools for different creative workflows, see our [2026 AI writing tools comparison](/blog/ai-writing-tools-comparison-2026).

## What Is Try AI Writer?

Try AI Writer is a versatile AI writing platform that handles everything from blog posts to creative fiction. It combines draft generation with SEO tools, tone controls, and a large template library. While it is not exclusively a creative writing tool, its flexible interface and tone adjustment features make it a solid option for fiction writers who also produce other types of content.

### Key Features

- **Article and story builder** with structured outlines and section-by-section generation
- **Tone adjustment slider** for shifting between genres and narrative voices
- **80+ content templates** including creative writing formats
- **Plagiarism detection** to ensure originality
- **Multi-language support** for writing in over 25 languages

## What Is Sudowrite?

Sudowrite is an AI writing tool designed specifically for fiction authors and creative writers. It offers features that no general-purpose tool matches — like story brainstorming, character development, world-building, and narrative expansion. Sudowrite understands story structure and helps you push through writer's block without taking over your creative vision.

### Key Features

- **Story Engine** that generates plot outlines, character arcs, and scene beats
- **Describe** feature that expands sensory details for settings and emotions
- **Rewrite** tool with options to rephrase, elaborate, or change the tone of selected text
- **Canvas** for organizing story elements, character sheets, and world-building notes
- **Shrink Ray** and **Expand** tools for adjusting scene pacing

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Fiction and Storytelling

Sudowrite is the clear winner for pure creative writing. Its Story Engine understands narrative structure — it can generate plot twists, develop character motivations, and suggest scene transitions that feel intentional rather than random. The Describe feature is particularly powerful for adding sensory detail without overwriting.

Try AI Writer can generate fiction, but it approaches it the same way it approaches any content type: prompt in, draft out. The output is readable but lacks the narrative nuance that Sudowrite provides. Try AI Writer does not offer character development tools, world-building canvases, or story structure guidance.

### Versatility

Try AI Writer wins on versatility. If you write fiction but also need blog posts, marketing emails, or social media content, Try AI Writer handles all of those from a single platform. Sudowrite is excellent for fiction but not designed for other content types. Using Sudowrite for a product description would be like using a race car for grocery runs — it works, but it is not the right tool.

### Editing and Revision

Sudowrite's Rewrite tool is one of the best revision features available. You select a passage and choose how you want it changed — more vivid, more concise, more formal, more emotional — and Sudowrite generates alternatives while preserving your voice. Try AI Writer's tone adjustment works at the document level, which is useful but less granular than Sudowrite's paragraph-level controls.

### World-Building and Organization

Sudowrite's Canvas feature lets you organize characters, locations, plot threads, and world-building details in one place. This is invaluable for novelists working on complex stories with many moving parts. Try AI Writer does not have an equivalent feature — it treats each document independently without story-level organization.

### Output Originality

Both tools produce original content, but Sudowrite's fiction-specific training means its creative output feels more unique and less formulaic. Try AI Writer's fiction can sometimes lean toward generic patterns, especially in genre fiction. The plagiarism checker in Try AI Writer is a nice safeguard, though originality is rarely an issue with either tool for creative writing.

## Pros and Cons

### Try AI Writer

**Pros:**
- More affordable pricing
- Versatile — handles fiction and non-fiction
- Built-in SEO tools for non-creative content
- Plagiarism detection included
- Larger template library for diverse content types

**Cons:**
- No story structure or character development tools
- Fiction output can feel generic
- No world-building or story organization features
- Revision tools are less granular

### Sudowrite

**Pros:**
- Purpose-built for fiction and creative writing
- Story Engine with plot and character guidance
- Excellent Describe and Rewrite tools
- Canvas for organizing complex stories
- Paragraph-level editing controls

**Cons:**
- Higher price point
- Not designed for non-fiction or marketing content
- Smaller user community and fewer integrations
- Overkill for casual or occasional fiction writing

## Which Tool Should You Choose?

**Choose Try AI Writer if:**
- You write fiction alongside other content types like blog posts and emails
- You want one tool for everything at an affordable price
- Your creative writing is occasional rather than your primary focus
- You need SEO and plagiarism tools for non-creative work

**Choose Sudowrite if:**
- You are a dedicated fiction writer who wants AI-powered story development
- You need tools for character creation, world-building, and plot structure
- You want paragraph-level revision controls for your narrative
- You are working on a novel, screenplay, or long-form creative project

## Final Verdict

For pure creative writing, Sudowrite is the superior tool. Its fiction-specific features — Story Engine, Describe, Canvas, and Rewrite — provide a creative writing experience that no general-purpose tool can match. However, if you are a multi-format creator who writes fiction as one part of a broader content strategy, Try AI Writer gives you more flexibility at a lower price. The right choice depends on whether fiction is your focus or just one of many content types you produce.
