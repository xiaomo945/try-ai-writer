The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) regulates advertising and marketing content. AI-generated content must comply with truth-in-advertising requirements like any other marketing material.

This guide covers FTC considerations for AI writing in marketing contexts.

## FTC Authority Overview

### Advertising Regulation Scope

FTC regulates advertising and marketing across media. AI-generated content falls within this scope when used for commercial purposes.

### Enforcement Powers

FTC enforces through guidance, warnings, consent decrees, and civil penalties. Violations can result in significant consequences.

### Key Principles

Truthfulness, substantiation, and non-deception form core FTC principles. AI marketing content must adhere to all three.

## Truth in Advertising Standards

### Material Representations

Advertisers must not make material representations that are false, misleading, or unsubstantiated. AI-generated content must meet these standards.

### Disclosure Requirements

Material connections between endorsers and advertisers must be disclosed. AI content involving endorsements requires appropriate disclosures.

### Substantiation Standards

Claims must be substantiated—supported by competent scientific evidence where applicable. AI content claiming specific benefits requires evidence.

## AI-Specific FTC Guidance

### AI Disclosure Considerations

FTC has indicated AI-generated content may require disclosure. Understand current guidance and monitor developments.

### Influencer and Endorsement Rules

AI-assisted influencer content must comply with endorsement guidelines. Clear relationships must be disclosed.

### Comparative Claims

AI-generated comparative claims must be substantiated. Ensure comparisons are accurate and defensible.

## Compliance Best Practices

### Human Oversight

Maintain human oversight of AI marketing content. FTC expects meaningful human involvement in advertising decisions.

### Review Processes

Implement review processes ensuring AI content meets FTC standards. Don't rely solely on AI for compliance.

### Documentation

Document compliance efforts. FTC expects evidence of good-faith compliance attempts.

## Handling Issues

### Self-Reporting Considerations

FTC encourages self-reporting of issues. Understand when and how to report potential violations.

### Consent Decrees

FTC consent decrees impose ongoing compliance obligations. Understand decree requirements if your organization receives one.

### Industry Guidance

FTC provides industry-specific guidance. Review guidance relevant to your industry and content types.
