## Introduction

Content creators have more AI writing tools than ever to choose from, and two names that come up constantly are Try AI Writer and Copy.ai. Both platforms help you generate content faster, but they were built with different philosophies. Try AI Writer focuses on long-form quality and SEO, while Copy.ai leans into short-form speed and marketing copy. This comparison breaks down everything you need to know to make the right call.

For a wider view of the AI writing landscape, check out our [2026 AI writing tools comparison](/blog/ai-writing-tools-comparison-2026).

## What Is Try AI Writer?

Try AI Writer is an AI writing platform designed for creators who want polished, SEO-friendly content without a steep learning curve. It combines draft generation with built-in optimization tools so you can write, refine, and publish from a single interface.

### Key Features

- **Long-form article builder** with structured outlines and section-by-section generation
- **Built-in SEO scoring** that provides real-time feedback on keywords and readability
- **Tone adjustment controls** for switching between formal, casual, and persuasive styles
- **Plagiarism detection** powered by an integrated checker
- **80+ content templates** covering blogs, emails, ads, and social media

## What Is Copy.ai?

Copy.ai started as a tool for generating marketing copy and has expanded into a broader AI writing platform. It is known for its massive template library and its focus on short-form, high-converting content like ad headlines, product descriptions, and email sequences.

### Key Features

- **900+ templates** for virtually every type of marketing copy
- **Workflows** that automate multi-step content creation processes
- **Brand voice customization** to maintain consistency across outputs
- **Sales and prospecting tools** including cold email and LinkedIn outreach generators
- **Team collaboration** with shared projects and approval flows

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Content Quality and Depth

Try AI Writer produces stronger long-form content. Its article builder creates structured, well-organized pieces that hold together from introduction to conclusion. The SEO integration means the content is already optimized by the time you finish editing.

Copy.ai excels at short-form copy. Its headlines, ad variations, and product descriptions are punchy and conversion-focused. However, when you push it into long-form territory, the output can feel disjointed — more like a series of disconnected paragraphs than a cohesive article.

### Template Variety

Copy.ai wins on sheer volume with over 900 templates. If you need a specific type of marketing copy — from Amazon product listings to Facebook ad variations — Copy.ai probably has a template for it. Try AI Writer has fewer templates but covers the most common content types with higher average quality per template.

### SEO and Optimization

Try AI Writer has a clear advantage here. Its built-in SEO scoring evaluates your content as you write, giving you a checklist of improvements. Copy.ai does not include native SEO tools, though you can integrate it with external platforms like Surfer SEO for an additional cost.

Learn more about how AI models handle optimization in our [Claude vs DeepSeek for writing](/blog/claude-vs-deepseek-for-writing) article.

### Workflow Automation

Copy.ai's Workflows feature is a standout. You can set up automated sequences that take a single input — like a product URL — and generate a full set of marketing assets: blog post, social captions, email sequence, and ad copy. Try AI Writer does not currently offer this level of automation, though its template system is efficient for manual content creation.

### Collaboration

Both tools support team collaboration, but Copy.ai's approval flows and shared project spaces are more developed for marketing teams. Try AI Writer offers real-time document collaboration that works well for small teams and co-writing scenarios.

## Pros and Cons

### Try AI Writer

**Pros:**
- Excellent long-form content quality
- Built-in SEO scoring at no extra cost
- Very affordable entry price
- Clean, focused writing interface
- Plagiarism detection included

**Cons:**
- Fewer templates than Copy.ai
- No workflow automation
- Limited marketing-specific copy tools

### Copy.ai

**Pros:**
- Massive template library for marketing copy
- Powerful Workflows for automated content generation
- Strong brand voice customization
- Built for marketing teams and agencies

**Cons:**
- Higher starting price
- Long-form content quality is inconsistent
- No built-in SEO tools
- Can feel overwhelming due to template volume

## Which Tool Should You Choose?

**Choose Try AI Writer if:**
- You write blog posts, articles, or other long-form content regularly
- You want SEO optimization built into your writing workflow
- You are a solo creator or small team on a budget
- You prefer quality over quantity in your templates

**Choose Copy.ai if:**
- You create marketing copy across many channels and formats
- You want to automate multi-step content workflows
- You need brand voice consistency across a team
- You write mostly short-form, conversion-focused content

## The Bottom Line

Try AI Writer and Copy.ai are both excellent tools, but they serve different primary use cases. Try AI Writer is the better choice for creators focused on long-form, SEO-optimized content at an affordable price. Copy.ai is the stronger pick for marketing teams that need to produce high volumes of short-form copy across multiple channels. Test both with your actual workflow to see which one fits naturally into your process.
