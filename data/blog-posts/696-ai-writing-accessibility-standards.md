Web accessibility ensures content usable by people with disabilities. AI writing tools can help create accessible content—or they can introduce accessibility barriers if used without awareness.

This guide covers accessibility considerations for AI writing workflows.

## Accessibility Fundamentals

### Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility serves users with disabilities, improves content for everyone, and increasingly becomes legal requirement. The business case and ethical case align.

### Disability Types Affecting Content

Various disabilities affect content consumption—visual impairments requiring screen readers, cognitive disabilities requiring clear language, motor impairments affecting navigation.

### Assistive Technologies

Screen readers, voice recognition, and alternative input devices help users with disabilities access content. Accessible content works with these technologies.

## Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

### WCAG Principles

WCAG establishes four principles—perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust. AI content should meet WCAG standards.

### Level A, AA, AAA

WCAG defines three conformance levels. Most organizations target Level AA as reasonable standard.

### Key Requirements

Text alternatives, keyboard navigation, clear language, and consistent structure support accessibility. AI writing can address these requirements.

## AI Writing Accessibility Features

### Alt Text Generation

AI can generate image alt text descriptions. Ensure AI-generated alt text meets quality standards.

### Clear Language

AI can simplify complex language for accessibility. Use these capabilities to improve content clarity.

### Structure Assistance

AI helps create logical content structures. Clear headings and organization benefit all users.

## Implementation Best Practices

### Template Accessibility

Build accessibility into content templates. AI-generated content from accessible templates inherits accessibility benefits.

### Review Processes

Human review catches accessibility issues AI misses. Include accessibility review in content workflows.

### User Testing

Users with disabilities provide invaluable accessibility feedback. Include disability community in testing processes.

## Specific Content Types

### Visual Content

Images require alt text. Video requires captions and transcripts. AI assists but human verification ensures quality.

### Complex Documents

PDFs and complex documents require specific accessibility treatments. AI helps create accessible document structures.

### Interactive Content

Forms and interactive elements require accessibility considerations. AI writing for interactive content needs accessibility awareness.
