Medium is one of the best platforms for writers who want to reach a built-in audience. With millions of readers and a curated distribution system, great content can find its way to thousands of eyes without any marketing spend.

But standing out on Medium requires more than good writing. You need headlines that earn curation, formatting that keeps readers engaged, and a strategy that builds momentum over time.

AI writing tools make it possible to publish consistently without sacrificing quality. Here's how to use them effectively on Medium.

## Understanding Medium's Curation System

Medium curators distribute content to topical feeds based on quality signals:

- **Engagement velocity**: How quickly content gets claps, comments, and shares
- **Read ratio**: How much of your article readers actually consume
- **Member reading time**: How long paying members spend on your content
- **Topic alignment**: How well your content matches Medium's publication topics

Understanding these signals helps you create content that gets distributed.

## The Importance of Headlines on Medium

Medium's headline is the single most important factor in getting clicks from the homepage, topic feeds, and email digests. Your headline determines:

- Whether curators notice your piece
- Whether readers click through
- How many people see your work

Great Medium headlines are:

- **Specific**: "5 Ways" beats "Tips" every time
- **Curiosity-driven**: Promise something valuable
- **Search-friendly**: Include terms people search for
- **Emotional**: Connect with reader desires or pain points

## Using AI to Write Medium Headlines

### Step 1: Know Your Angle

Before AI, determine:

- What's the main promise or insight?
- Who is your target reader?
- What format works best (list, how-to, story, etc.)?

### Step 2: Generate Headline Options

Ask AI for 10-15 headline variations. Provide:

- Your article topic
- Target format
- Desired tone
- Primary keyword or phrase

### Step 3: Evaluate and Select

Choose headlines that are:

- Specific and concrete
- Benefit-focused
- Free of clickbait
- Honest about content

### Step 4: Test Subtitles

Medium allows subtitles. Generate 5 options that expand on your headline without repeating it.

## Medium Article Formatting Best Practices

### Use Headers Liberally

Break your article into scannable sections. H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections.

### Keep Paragraphs Short

2-4 sentences per paragraph. Readers on mobile need brevity.

### Use Lists

Numbered and bulleted lists improve scannability and often get higher engagement.

### Add Block Quotes

Quote memorable lines from your piece or from sources. They break up text visually.

### Include Images

Cover images and in-article images boost engagement significantly.

## AI for Article Body Generation

### Step 1: Create an Outline

Generate a detailed outline based on your topic. Include:

- Target length (Medium articles work well at 1,500-4,000 words)
- Key points to cover
- Examples or case studies to include

### Step 2: Draft Sections

Generate content section by section rather than all at once. This allows for better quality control.

### Step 3: Add Your Voice

AI drafts are foundations. Add:

- Personal anecdotes
- Specific examples
- Your unique perspective
- Conversational asides

### Step 4: Edit for Medium Style

Medium readers appreciate:

- Direct language
- Short sentences
- Active voice
- Conversational tone

## Content Types That Perform Well on Medium

### Personal Essays

Stories with lessons resonate deeply. AI can help structure, but the emotion must be yours.

### How-To Guides

Practical, actionable content that delivers on its promise gets saved and shared.

### List Articles

"Lists of X" are consistently high performers when the items are genuinely useful.

### Thought Leadership

Hot takes and contrarian perspectives generate engagement, even controversy.

### Case Studies

Real examples with specific results provide credibility and value.

## Building a Publication Strategy

### Consistency Matters

Publish regularly—weekly if possible. Medium's algorithm favors active writers.

### Series and Themes

Create recurring series that encourage followers. AI can help generate continuation ideas.

### Publication vs. Profile

Consider submitting to Medium publications for additional distribution. AI can help adapt content for different publication styles.

### Cross-Promotion

Share your Medium articles on LinkedIn, Twitter, and in newsletters. AI can help create platform-specific excerpts.

## Best Practices for Medium Success

### Hook in the First 3 Sentences

Most readers decide within seconds whether to continue. Make them count.

### Deliver on Your Promise

Clickbait gets clicks but damages reputation. Always deliver what your headline promises.

### End with a CTA

Ask readers to:

- Follow you
- Comment their thoughts
- Read related articles
- Share if they found it valuable

### Respond to Responses

Engagement with readers builds community and signals quality to Medium's algorithm.

### Optimize for Member Reading

Paying members are Medium's priority. Content that keeps them reading gets distributed more.

## Common Medium Mistakes

### Posting Without Headline Testing

Your headline deserves attention. Generate multiple options and choose strategically.

### Ignoring Formatting

Unstructured walls of text get abandoned. Format for your readers.

### Being Too Promotional

Medium readers value authentic content. Promotional pieces get muted.

### Inconsistent Quality

One great article isn't enough. Build with consistent output.

### Ignoring Analytics

Medium provides data on what's working. Use it to refine your approach.

## Measuring Medium Performance

Track:

- **Views**: Total readers who saw your piece
- **Reads**: How many actually read (read ratio matters)
- **Claps**: Engagement signal
- **Responses**: Comments and discussions
- **Follower growth**: Are you building an audience?

## Final Thoughts

Medium rewards writers who provide consistent, valuable content. AI helps you produce more without burning out, but your expertise and voice remain essential.
