# AI Writing for SMS Marketing: 160 Characters to Convert

SMS marketing is the most intimate digital channel. Your message lands directly on someone's phone — the device they check 96 times per day. That intimacy is both the opportunity and the risk. Done right, SMS drives the highest engagement rates of any marketing channel, with open rates exceeding 98% and click-through rates averaging 19%. Done wrong, it's the fastest way to lose a subscriber permanently.

The challenge is extreme brevity. You have 160 characters to capture attention, communicate value, and drive action. Every character must justify its existence. AI writing tools can help you generate, test, and optimize SMS copy within these constraints — but the discipline of brevity requires human judgment about what matters most.

## The SMS Writing Challenge

SMS marketing writing has unique constraints that differentiate it from every other content type.

### Extreme Character Constraints

160 characters isn't a suggestion — it's a hard limit. Exceed it and your message gets split into multiple segments, which looks unprofessional and can confuse recipients. Every word, every space, every punctuation mark must be intentional. AI can generate concise copy, but you must specify character limits explicitly and count carefully.

### The Permission Economy

SMS is an opt-in channel. Every recipient has given you permission to message them, and they can revoke that permission with a single reply of "STOP." This means every message must deliver value. If you send messages that feel spammy, irrelevant, or excessive, you'll lose subscribers faster than you gain them.

### Immediate Consumption

Unlike email, which might sit in an inbox for hours, SMS messages are typically read within 3 minutes of receipt. This immediacy is powerful for time-sensitive offers and urgent updates, but it means your copy must be instantly clear — there's no time for the reader to figure out what you're trying to say.

## SMS Copy Frameworks for AI

These frameworks give AI the structure it needs to produce effective SMS copy within character constraints.

### The Offer-Deadline-CTA Framework

The most reliable SMS structure: state the offer, create urgency with a deadline, and provide a clear call to action. "30% off all plans. Ends midnight. Shop now: [link]" This structure works because it delivers maximum information in minimum characters.

Prompt AI: "Write an SMS marketing message for [offer]. Include: the offer, a deadline for urgency, and a clear CTA with link placeholder. Maximum 140 characters (leaving room for links). Tone: urgent but not aggressive."

### The Value-CTA Framework

When you're not making a direct offer, lead with the value the message provides. "Your weekly report is ready. See your top 3 insights: [link]" This framework works for informational messages that keep subscribers engaged between promotional messages.

### The Question-CTA Framework

Questions increase engagement by prompting mental participation. "Ready to upgrade your workflow? See what's new: [link]" The question creates a micro-commitment that makes the reader more likely to click.

## Optimizing SMS Copy with AI

Beyond initial drafting, AI can help optimize your SMS copy for better performance.

### Character Count Optimization

AI can help compress messages to fit within character limits without losing key information. "Compress this SMS message to under 140 characters while preserving the offer, deadline, and CTA: [original message]." Compression often produces tighter, more impactful copy.

### A/B Test Generation

Generate multiple variations for A/B testing. Test different offers, different urgency triggers, different CTAs, and different personalization approaches. AI can produce 10-15 variations in seconds, giving you a robust testing pool.

Prompt AI: "Generate 10 variations of this SMS message, each testing a different element: 3 with different urgency triggers, 3 with different CTAs, 2 with different personalization approaches, and 2 with different offer framings. All must be under 140 characters."

### Personalization at Scale

AI can help generate personalized SMS variations based on customer segments. "Write SMS messages for three customer segments: new subscribers, repeat buyers, and lapsed customers. Each message should reference their relationship stage and offer relevant value."

## SMS Marketing Best Practices

Follow these guidelines to maximize the effectiveness of your AI-generated SMS campaigns.

### Frequency Discipline

The number one reason people unsubscribe from SMS is too many messages. Most brands should send no more than 2-4 messages per month. Every message must deliver clear value. If you're sending "just to stay in touch," you're burning subscriber goodwill.

### Timing Optimization

Send messages when recipients are most likely to engage. For most audiences, this means weekday late mornings or early afternoons. Avoid early mornings, late nights, and weekends unless your audience specifically expects it. AI can help you test different send times and identify optimal windows.

### Clear Opt-Out

Every message should include a clear opt-out instruction, typically "Reply STOP to unsubscribe." This isn't just best practice — it's legally required in most jurisdictions. AI can help integrate opt-out language naturally without wasting too many characters.

### Compliance Awareness

SMS marketing is heavily regulated. The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) in the US, GDPR in Europe, and similar regulations worldwide impose strict requirements on consent, disclosure, and opt-out processing. AI can help draft compliant copy, but legal review is essential.

## Practical Tips for AI SMS Writing

1. **Always specify character limits in prompts.** AI will overrun limits unless explicitly constrained.
2. **Leave room for links and tracking parameters.** Links can consume 20-30 characters. Plan for this.
3. **Use URL shorteners.** Every character counts. Shortened URLs save valuable space.
4. **Test on actual devices.** Messages can render differently on different phones. Preview before sending.
5. **Generate multiple variations for every send.** A/B testing is essential for optimizing SMS performance.
6. **Track unsubscribe rates by message type.** Identify which messages drive unsubscribes and adjust your strategy.
7. **Keep a swipe file of high-performing messages.** Feed these to AI as examples when generating new copy.
