# AI Writing for PR: Craft Press Releases and Media Pitches

Public relations is a credibility game. Your press release competes with hundreds of others for a journalist's attention, and your media pitch has about three seconds to prove it's worth reading. In an industry where relationships and reputation are everything, AI writing tools can help you produce more polished, targeted PR content — but only if you use them strategically.

The temptation with AI in PR is to automate everything: generate press releases at scale, blast generic pitches to massive media lists, and hope quantity produces quality. That approach fails because it ignores what makes PR work: relevance, timeliness, and genuine news value. This guide shows you how to use AI to enhance your PR writing while maintaining the credibility and personalization that earn media coverage.

## The PR Writing Challenge

PR writing has unique demands that differentiate it from marketing copy, blog content, or advertising. Understanding these demands is essential for effective AI-assisted PR.

### The Credibility Filter

Journalists are professional skeptics. They evaluate every pitch and press release through a credibility filter. Is this actually news? Is the source trustworthy? Is this worth my readers' time? AI-generated PR content that feels generic, exaggerated, or self-serving gets filtered out immediately. Your AI writing must pass the credibility test that every human-written piece faces.

### The Newsworthiness Standard

Not everything your company does is newsworthy. AI can help you write about anything, but it can't tell you what deserves coverage. The judgment call about newsworthiness remains a human responsibility. Before using AI to write a press release, ask: would a journalist at a reputable outlet care about this? If the honest answer is no, no amount of AI-generated polish will make it newsworthy.

### The Personalization Requirement

Effective media pitches are personalized. They reference the journalist's previous work, explain why this story is relevant to their beat, and suggest a specific angle that fits their publication. AI can help with personalization at scale, but the initial research and relationship understanding must come from you.

## Writing Press Releases with AI

Press releases follow a specific format that AI handles well when given proper guidance. Here's how to produce AI-generated press releases that journalists will actually read.

### The Inverted Pyramid Structure

Press releases lead with the most important information and add detail in descending order of importance. The first paragraph should answer who, what, when, where, and why. Subsequent paragraphs add context, quotes, and background. AI can execute this structure reliably when you provide the key facts upfront.

Prompt AI: "Write a press release using inverted pyramid structure. Lead with: [company] announces [news] on [date]. Key facts: [list 3-5 facts]. Include a quote from [spokesperson] saying [key message]. Background: [company description]."

### The Quote That Adds Value

Quotes in press releases should say something meaningful, not just restate the headline in first person. AI tends to generate generic quotes like "We're excited to announce..." Instead, prompt for quotes that add perspective, context, or vision. "Write a quote from the CEO that explains why this matters for the industry, not just the company."

### The Boilerplate That Differentiates

The company boilerplate at the end of a press release is often an afterthought, but it's where journalists go to understand who you are. AI can generate boilerplates, but make sure yours differentiates your company rather than describing it in generic terms. ## Writing Media Pitches with AI

Media pitches require a different approach than press releases. They're shorter, more personal, and more persuasive. Here's how to use AI for pitches that get responses.

### The Subject Line

Your pitch subject line determines whether the email gets opened. It should be specific, timely, and relevant to the journalist's beat. "Story idea: How [trend] is changing [industry]" works better than "Press inquiry." Prompt AI to generate 10-15 subject line variations and test the most compelling ones.

### The Opening Hook

The first sentence of your pitch must demonstrate that you know the journalist's work and have a story relevant to their audience. "Your recent piece on [topic] highlighted [point]. I have a new angle that builds on this..." AI can help craft these hooks when you provide the journalist's recent work as context.

### The Story Proposition

Clearly state the story you're proposing and why it matters now. Timeliness is critical — journalists want stories that are relevant today, not next month. "With [recent event/trend], [your story angle] offers a timely perspective that your readers need." AI can help articulate the timeliness angle when you provide the relevant context.

### The Proof Points

Include 2-3 specific data points or expert sources that make the story credible and easy to write. Journalists are more likely to cover stories where the research is already done. AI can help organize and present your proof points clearly.

## AI PR Writing Best Practices

1. **Always customize pitches for each journalist.** AI can help with personalization, but never send the same generic pitch to multiple reporters.
2. **Keep press releases under 400 words.** Journalists scan; they don't read. Brevity signals that you respect their time.
3. **Include specific data, not vague claims.** "Grew revenue 47%" is credible. "Experienced significant growth" is not.
4. **Write headlines that read like news, not marketing.** "Company Launches Product" is marketing. "New Tool Cuts Content Production Time by 60%" is news.
5. **Fact-check every AI-generated claim.** AI can fabricate statistics and misattribute quotes. Verify everything before sending.
6. **Maintain a consistent brand voice across all PR materials.** Create a voice guide that you include in every AI prompt.
7. **Follow up with genuine value, not just reminders.** AI can help draft follow-up emails that offer additional information or sources rather than just asking "did you get my pitch?"

## Conclusion

AI writing tools can significantly improve the efficiency and quality of your PR communications, but they can't replace the strategic judgment that makes PR effective. The best approach combines AI's content generation speed with human oversight on newsworthiness, personalization, and credibility. Use AI to draft, iterate, and polish — but let human judgment guide what you pitch, to whom, and when.
