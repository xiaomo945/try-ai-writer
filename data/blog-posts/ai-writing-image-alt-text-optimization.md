# AI Writing for Image Alt Text: SEO Best Practices

Image alt text is one of the simplest yet most overlooked SEO opportunities. Every image on your website is a chance to rank in image search, improve accessibility, and provide additional context to search engines. Yet most content creators either skip alt text entirely or write generic descriptions that add no value.

AI writing tools are perfectly suited for alt text generation. They can analyze images and produce descriptive, keyword-rich alt text at scale. Here is how to use AI writing for alt text that actually improves your SEO.

## Why Alt Text Matters for SEO

### Image Search Visibility

Google Images accounts for over 20% of all web search traffic. Without descriptive alt text, your images are essentially invisible to search engines. Alt text is the primary signal Google uses to understand and index images.

### Accessibility Compliance

Alt text is essential for screen readers used by visually impaired users. The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) require alt text for all informative images. Good alt text serves both SEO and accessibility goals.

### Contextual Signals for Page Content

Images with relevant alt text reinforce the topical relevance of your page. When your alt text includes keywords related to your page content, it provides additional signals that help search engines understand what the page is about.

### AI Search and Image Understanding

AI search engines are increasingly capable of analyzing images, but they still rely heavily on alt text and surrounding context. Well-written alt text increases the chances that your images — and the pages they appear on — are surfaced in AI-generated answers.

## What Makes Good Alt Text

### Descriptive and Specific

Good alt text describes what is actually in the image. Instead of "chart," write "bar chart showing AI writing tool adoption rates from 2023 to 2026." Specificity helps both search engines and users.

### Concise but Complete

Aim for 80-125 characters. Long enough to be descriptive, short enough to be digestible. Screen readers typically stop reading alt text after about 125 characters.

### Keyword-Relevant Without Stuffing

Include relevant keywords naturally, but do not force them. Alt text that reads like a keyword list is bad for both accessibility and SEO. Google can detect keyword stuffing in alt text just as it can in body content.

### Contextually Appropriate

The same image might need different alt text depending on the context. A screenshot of an AI writing tool might be described differently in a tutorial versus a comparison review.

### Unique Across Your Site

Avoid using the same alt text for multiple images. Each image is unique, and your alt text should reflect that uniqueness.

## Using AI Writing Tools for Alt Text

### Automated Alt Text Generation

AI writing tools can generate alt text automatically based on image analysis and page context. This is especially valuable for sites with large image libraries.

### Best Practices for AI-Generated Alt Text

#### Provide Context in Your Prompts

The more context you give AI tools, the better the alt text:

- "Write alt text for a screenshot of the Try AI Writer dashboard showing the content editor with an SEO optimization panel"
- "Create alt text for an infographic comparing AI writing tool features across five platforms"
- "Generate alt text for a photo of a content team collaborating around a laptop with AI writing software on screen"

#### Include Target Keywords

Mention your target keywords in the prompt so the AI can incorporate them naturally:

- "Write alt text that includes the phrase 'AI writing tools' for an image showing..."
- "Create alt text mentioning 'content optimization' for a diagram of..."

#### Specify the Level of Detail

Tell the AI how detailed the alt text should be:

- Brief: 50-80 characters for decorative or simple images
- Standard: 80-125 characters for most content images
- Detailed: 125-250 characters for complex infographics or data visualizations

#### Review and Refine

Always review AI-generated alt text before publishing. Check for:

- Accuracy: Does the alt text accurately describe the image?
- Relevance: Does it include appropriate keywords naturally?
- Conciseness: Is it the right length for the image type?
- Uniqueness: Is it different from alt text on other images?

## Alt Text Strategies by Image Type

### Screenshots and UI Images

Screenshots of software, apps, and websites are common in tech content:

- Name the software or interface shown
- Describe the specific feature or section visible
- Include the purpose or action being demonstrated
- Example: "Try AI Writer content editor showing SEO score of 92 with keyword density panel"

### Infographics and Data Visualizations

Data images need alt text that conveys the key information:

- State the type of visualization (bar chart, pie chart, flow diagram)
- Summarize the main finding or trend
- Include relevant data points
- Consider providing a full text alternative below the image for complex infographics

### Product Images

E-commerce and review content uses product images:

- Name the product specifically
- Describe key visible features
- Include model numbers or versions when relevant
- Example: "Try AI Writer Pro subscription dashboard showing brand voice settings panel"

### Photos and Illustrations

Contextual photos need descriptive but concise alt text:

- Describe what is happening in the photo
- Include relevant people, places, or objects
- Mention the setting or context
- Example: "Content marketer reviewing AI-generated blog post on laptop at standing desk"

### Decorative Images

Images that are purely decorative and add no informational value should have empty alt attributes (alt=""). This tells screen readers to skip them. Do not use AI to generate alt text for decorative images.

## Common Alt Text Mistakes

### Leaving Alt Text Empty

Every informative image needs alt text. An empty alt attribute on a meaningful image is a missed SEO opportunity and an accessibility failure.

### Using the Image Filename as Alt Text

File names like "IMG_3847.jpg" or "screenshot-2026-05-30" provide no useful information. Always write descriptive alt text regardless of the filename.

### Keyword Stuffing

Alt text like "AI writing tool AI writer best AI writing software AI content generator" is spammy and counterproductive. Write natural descriptions that happen to include relevant keywords.

### Being Too Vague

Alt text like "image" or "chart" tells search engines nothing useful. Be specific about what the image shows and why it matters.

### Duplicating the Caption

If an image has a visible caption, the alt text should provide additional detail, not repeat the same information. The caption serves sighted users; the alt text serves screen readers and search engines.

## Scaling Alt Text with AI Workflows

### Batch Processing

For sites with hundreds or thousands of images, use AI tools to generate alt text in batches:

1. Export a list of all images missing alt text
2. Provide page context and target keywords for each image
3. Generate alt text for all images at once
4. Review and refine the generated alt text
5. Update your CMS with the new alt text

### Integration with Content Creation

Build alt text generation into your content creation workflow:

- When AI writes a blog post, it should also generate alt text for every image
- Include alt text specifications in your content briefs
- Make alt text a required field in your CMS
- Audit alt text during content reviews

### Ongoing Alt Text Audits

Regularly audit your site for missing or poor alt text:

- Use crawling tools to find images without alt text
- Check for generic or duplicate alt text
- Verify that alt text includes relevant keywords
- Update alt text when content or context changes

## Measuring Alt Text SEO Impact

Track these metrics to evaluate your alt text optimization:

- **Image search impressions and clicks**: Monitor Google Search Console image data
- **Image ranking positions**: Track your images for target queries in Google Images
- **Accessibility audit scores**: Use tools like Lighthouse to measure WCAG compliance
- **Overall page rankings**: Improved alt text can boost page-level rankings through better topical signals
