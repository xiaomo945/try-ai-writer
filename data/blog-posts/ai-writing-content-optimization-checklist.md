Publishing AI-generated content without proper review invites embarrassing errors, inconsistent messaging, and missed opportunities. This checklist ensures every piece meets professional standards before reaching your audience. Use it as a systematic review process that catches common issues before they damage your reputation.

## Pre-Publication Review Framework

Before diving into specific checklist items, establish a review mindset. Effective content review separates the editing process into distinct passes, each focusing on specific concerns. Trying to catch everything in one read-through leads to missed errors.

Common review passes include:

- **Accuracy review**: Fact-checking, claims verification, outdated information updates
- **Voice and tone review**: Brand consistency, readability, engagement quality
- **Technical review**: Grammar, spelling, punctuation, formatting
- **SEO review**: Keyword integration, meta elements, structural optimization
- **Final polish**: Reading flow, call-to-action effectiveness, overall impact

Each pass addresses specific quality dimensions without overwhelming reviewers.

## Accuracy and Fact-Checking

AI generates content based on training data, which may be outdated or incorrect for your specific context. Verify all factual claims before publishing.

### Verify Statistics and Data

Every statistic, percentage, or numerical claim requires independent verification. AI may generate plausible-sounding but inaccurate numbers. Search for primary sources and update figures to reflect current data.

Check that cited facts haven't changed since the AI's training cutoff. Industry statistics, market figures, and research findings evolve constantly. Confirm that supporting evidence remains accurate and current.

### Review Industry-Specific Information

Technical content, especially in fast-moving fields, requires careful review. AI may reference outdated technologies, superseded best practices, or deprecated tools. Subject matter experts should review technical accuracy before publication.

### Update Time-Sensitive Content

Remove or update any references to dates, events, or circumstances that may have changed. Content referencing "last year" or "recently" needs temporal verification. Build in review dates for evergreen content that might require periodic updates.

## Voice and Tone Consistency

Your brand voice should remain consistent whether human or AI created the initial draft. Review content against established voice guidelines.

### Check Against Brand Style Guide

Reference your brand style guide during review. Does the content reflect your defined voice characteristics? Verify consistent formality level, vocabulary choices, and communication style.

### Evaluate Readability

Read the full piece aloud. Does it flow naturally? Would your target audience find it engaging and accessible? AI content sometimes reads stiffly or includes unnecessarily complex constructions that human writers would simplify.

### Assess Emotional Impact

What should readers feel after consuming this content? Does the piece achieve that emotional response? Review headlines, introductions, and conclusions specifically for their emotional effectiveness.

## SEO and Discoverability

Even excellent content fails if audiences cannot find it. Review SEO elements carefully.

### Title and Meta Elements

- Primary keyword appears in the title
- Title is compelling and click-worthy
- Meta description summarizes content accurately and includes relevant keywords
- URL slug is clean and descriptive

### Structural Optimization

- Primary keyword appears in at least one H2 heading
- Content uses hierarchical heading structure (H2, H3, etc.)
- Headings accurately describe following content
- Paragraphs are appropriately sized for readability

### Internal and External Links

- Links go to authoritative, relevant sources
- Internal links connect to related content on your site
- Anchor text describes link destinations accurately
- All links function correctly

## Technical Quality Review

Fundamentals matter. Technical errors undermine credibility even when the underlying content is valuable.

### Grammar and Mechanics

- Subject-verb agreement is consistent
- Verb tenses remain consistent or shift appropriately
- Pronoun references are clear
- Commas and other punctuation are correct

### Spelling and Word Choice

- No spelling errors (including industry terminology)
- Correct word usage (their/there/they're, affect/effect, etc.)
- Consistent terminology throughout
- No instances of repeated or missing words

### Formatting Consistency

- Bullet points follow consistent style
- Numbered lists use parallel structure
- Headings follow consistent capitalization
- Consistent use of bold and italic formatting

## Visual and Multimedia Elements

Text alone rarely maximizes content impact. Review supporting elements thoughtfully.

### Image Quality and Relevance

- Images relate directly to content
- Alt text accurately describes images
- Images have appropriate licenses or are original
- Image sizes are optimized for web performance

### Additional Resources

- Are tables or charts clearly formatted?
- Do embedded videos enhance understanding?
- Is there appropriate whitespace for readability?

## Call-to-Action Review

Every content piece should have a purpose beyond information delivery. Define the desired action and evaluate whether the content supports it.

### CTA Effectiveness

- A clear call-to-action exists
- The CTA relates logically to content
- CTA language is compelling and action-oriented
- CTA placement draws appropriate attention

## Final Quality Gates

Before publishing, conduct these final checks:

- Content meets minimum quality standards for your publication
- No placeholder text or incomplete sections remain
- Preview mode reveals any formatting issues
- Mobile display renders correctly
- Page load time is acceptable
